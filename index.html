Dominant 7th vs Diminished 7th
A Dominant 7th (G7) has a major 3rd and a minor 7th — it creates tension that wants to resolve to the I chord.

A Diminished 7th (Bdim7) stacks four equal minor thirds. All intervals are 3 semitones apart — completely symmetric and spooky.
G7: G B (M3) D (P5) F (m7)
Bdim7: B D (m3) F (d5) Ab (d7)
The number system
Chord progressions are written as numbers (1-6-2-5). Each number is a chord built on that scale degree. In C major: 1=C, 2=Dm, 3=Em, 4=F, 5=G, 6=Am. Move the shapes to any key instantly.
